Estimates & Workflow

Start a new estimate, add the project info, then work the same sequence every time: Materials → Labor (units & adjustments) → DJE → Summary. Consistency prevents misses. Save anytime; export when ready.

Open an estimate and choose Edit, Clone, or Delete. Cloning is best for similar scopes—rename it and adjust quantities, not just prices.

Your defaults (rates, overhead, margin, tax) carry over to new estimates. Line items don’t. Each project is independent to avoid cross-contamination.

Materials, DJE & Assemblies

Materials: Individual items you purchase and install.
DJE (Direct Job Expense): Job-specific costs that aren’t materials (permits, equipment, travel).
Assemblies: A reusable bundle (materials + labor) you add in one step for repeat scopes.

A labor unit is the time to install one item under standard conditions. Adjust when job conditions change (height, access, schedule, repetition, crew skill). The app totals quantity × labor unit × any adjustment.

Troubleshooting

Start from the Estimates page (not a reused tab) and confirm you didn’t open a clone by mistake. If it still looks off, save the estimate name, note your steps, and email support so we can help.

Save the estimate first, then export. If the issue continues, share the estimate name, time of attempt, and browser with support.

Hard-refresh your browser, close other heavy tabs, and update to the latest version. If it continues, contact support with your browser and OS version.

Keep the base scope clean. Price alternates separately so clients can compare. For changes after award, create a change order that references the original estimate line(s) and shows the delta clearly.
